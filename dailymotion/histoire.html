L'histoire

Tribute to G. I. Gurdjieff

  • 1912

  • Gurdjieff Attracts First Followers

    January 1

    Upon returning to Moscow after a prolonged period of travelling abroard, Gurdjieff attracted his first students.

  • 1919

  • Moving to Georgia (The Country)

    Unknown

    Gurdjieff and his closest pupils moved to Tifilis, Georgia. For a time, Gurdjieff worked on a ballet titled The Struggle of the Magicians, while he continued to take on more students.

  • 1921

  • The Institute for the Harmonious Development of Man

    Unknown

    After a time in Georgia and Turkey, Gurdjieff travelled to western Europe, giving demonstrations of his work in various cities. Upon a unsuccessful attempt to gain British citizenship, Gurdjieff established the Institute for the Harmonious Development of Man in Paris, France.

  • 1924

  • All and Everything

    Unknown

    Gurdjieff began writing Beelzebub's Tales, the first part of the All and Everything series.

  • 1935

  • All and Some Things

    Unknown

    Gurdjieff stopped work on his All and Everything series. The first two parts of the trilogy were complete, but the third was not. The third part of the trilogy was later published under the title Life Is Real Only Then, When 'I Am'.

  • 1936

  • Fading

    Unknown

    Gurdjieff taught during World War II in a flat at 6 Rue des Colonels-Renard. At this point in his life, his teaching was far removed from the original system-- although his pupils were required to read Beelzebub's Tales three times.
